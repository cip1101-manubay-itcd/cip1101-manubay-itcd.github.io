Three Periods: Me, In Essence

Written By: Chazz C. Manubay
CIP 1101 - ITCD

My Childhood Years (2001 - 2014)

Who in the world am I? Ah, that's the great puzzle.
— Lewis Carroll, Alice in Wonderland

        I was born in Marikina, though most of my childhood memories took place in San Mateo, Rizal.1 It was there where my family lived for a long time, because my older siblings had to attend school there.2 We weren't blessed with wealth, nor a complete family.3 But thanks to our relatives overseas, and to my mother who worked herself to the bone, we didn't have to let a day pass without eating.4 My environment there was nice.5 Our neighbors were very kind, and based on the stories, they loved seeing me run all over the neighborhood.6 My cousins also visited our house at times, so we were able to play.7 Some of my cousins moved into the neighborhood as well, so we saw each other more frequently.8 Together, we made friends with other kids in the neighborhood.9 One unforgettable memory that I have was the first time that I got to go to the river.10 Not just the bridge, but the actual waters of the river.11 I was happy just to be able to actually feel the stream across my legs.12 It was also in my childhood that I learned how to ride a bike, though my friend from my school had to scare me by chasing me so that I could keep pedaling.13 I also had fun in school.14 I no longer remember memories from nursery up to preparatory school, other than the daily commute with my mom to my school.15 I remember we would stop at a Pandayan branch in Marikina for her to buy me snacks and drinks to bring to school.16 After preparatory school, I started first grade in the same school as my older siblings.17 It was a new environment, but I managed to fit in properly.18 The funniest memory I have from my early grade school is when I didn't like my Mathematics teacher so I reported her directly to the Student Affairs Office.19 My mom was called to school because of that, and I think she talked to the teacher and then things were great afterwards.20

        A lot of things happened midway.21 I had to study in a public school, and I had my worst experience there.22 A classmate of mine accidentally cut my palm with a bamboo stick, and I had to get it stitched.23 After that year, I went back to my old school.24 My late grade school memories was my obsession with Geronimo Stilton books.25 They were easy to read for me, and the stories were very imaginative.26 I remember my library debt piling up because I take too long before I returned them.27 Also, when I was in fifth grade, that was the time that I started to question my sexuality.28 In sixth grade, my family had to move to Marikina, so I changed schools during my last school year.29 It was again a new environment, and this time I found it harder to fit in because the culture was different from my old school.30 To make things harder, I was elected as the section president.31 Leading wasn't something I am capable of during the time, so our section had the reputation of being the worst and lawless.32 It was so bad that my section adviser had to disregard the entire set of class officers.33 Though my position remained, it was no longer good.34 That was then I realized that I was missing out on a lot of fun.35 When that happened, I started to make a lot of friends within my section, and then started to resolve conflicts between me and other classmates little-by-little.36 I am happy that it went well towards the end, given that it was the end of my childhood and the start of my teenage life.37

My Teenage Years (2014 - 2021)

Your time is limited, so don't waste it living someone else's life. Don't be trapped by dogma—which is living with the results of other people's thinking.
— Steve Jobs

        My teenage life started halfway through seventh grade.1 This was a period of my life wherein I encountered a lot of changes.2 Puberty was one thing, but there have also been changes with the way I think, the way I feel about others, and the way I see myself.3 I wasn't very focused on studying during my early teenage years.4 Instead, I was studying for the purpose of making friends with a lot of people.5 By that time, I have already established friendship with several people.6 I also discovered my passion for singing, and learned the most basic way of playing guitar.7 By eigth grade, I found myself in a relationship with my first official girlfriend.8 I learned a lot of things from her, and although it was days short of an anniversary, we separated in good terms.9 After that, I became more and more confused with my sexuality.10 I didn't know if I was heterosexual, homosexual, or bisexual.11 That lasted for quite a long time, and I started being infatuated towards multiple people, male and female.12 By ninth grade, I started studying seriously.13 I became hard on myself because I didn't want to slack off, especially since there were also problems regarding my family's financial stability.14 I then realized that I had to balance things out.15 So in tenth grade, I studied seriously but still made time for fun with friends.16 In those last two years of Junior High School, I was active at a Christian church where my friends invited me.17 It was fun for the most part, I made church friends and had fun with them as well.18

        I moved up from Junior High School with a fairly high grade, and I had to leave my school to study at UE for Senior High School.19 It was a new environment, but at this point I knew that it didn't matter much if I didn't fit in, and since I've learned how to socialize with people, I could easily talk to them.20 This time, I was very focused with studying.21 It was a way for me to redirect my stress and bitterness from having to move to another school while my friends stayed.22 In my last year of Senior High School, I met my first official boyfriend.23 It was also this time that I have made my sexuality clear: I am pansexual.24 I had fun, because I got to live my authentic self.25 Of course, my friends from my previous school have also heard the news.26 It was also the time that I officially came out to my family, and they have fully accepted me.27 I finished Senior High School with honors, as well as with new friends that I still talk to until now.28 My relationship with my boyfriend was also days short of an anniversary, but it peacefully ended after a long period of uncertainty.29 I've learned a lot about myself during Senior High School, especially the fact that I have been hurt by a lot of things that happened to me in the past.30 A lot of things happened during these two years, and while most of them have been instrumental to my growth, some of them have been quite detrimental.31

My College Years (2021 - Present)

There is nothing permanent except change.
— Heraclitus

        My college years have been a change of pace.1 The pandemic started right before my first year of college, so my experience is very unique from other batches of students.2 I put myself under so much pressure, so that I'll always do better everytime I get the chance to.3 I've also been isolated with my thoughts, and they don't help me much.4 I am much happier in college though, because I am much more focused on things that I am interested in.5 Even if this program is only second to the one that I wanted most, I still feel like I will do good.6 I also cannot believe that I will easily make friends.7 My childhood friend also became my classmate, for the first time.8 It was a funny story because we've known each other for a while but we never became classmates back in Junior High School.9 Well, I am happy that he is my classmate now.10 I also am classmates once again with a friend from Senior High School, and I cannot believe that our friendship will go deeper.11 That was because back then, we did not belong to the same circle, and we only had a quality moment when we were grouped together for our research.12 Nonetheless, she is a great friend.13 I have also made friends with three other graduates from UE's Basic Education Department.14 Though, one of us decided that BSIT is not the course he should be taking, so he moved to another university and took up something related to health sciences.15

        From six, we became five.16 Don't get me wrong, us six still have a strong connection and always keeps in touch.17 I've also made friends with people I didn't think I would be friends with.18 And it's great that I got to see some of them in person already.19 It is a healthy relationship, if I were to be asked.20 We would all be of help to each other whenever one of us needs it.21 And everyone would go out of their way just to keep everyone in check.22 It's like an unwritten rule of accountability.23 Others treat college life as an individual battleground, but we see it as an opportunity for cooperation.24 I've also met outstanding professors who were very passionate to teach.25 Yes, Prof. Ventura. You are one of them.26 While I also had my share of professors who I didn't see eye-to-eye with, I still know and understand that they have their personal struggles.27 Nonetheless, a minor inconvenience will do little to no damage on my passion for learning.28 I am still almost halfway through college, so I still have not much to say.29 All I know is that I am having fun with the people around me so far.30 I hope to keep that happiness around until we graduate and go about the rest of our lives.31 I sure hope that the relationships I built at this point will not be easily disposable.32
